## Dry brine (1 hr+ ahead)
1. Sprinkle both sides of the duck with the salt, sugar and five-spice. Leave for [at least an hour](timer:1h), ideally eight hours or more:
   - 2 duck breasts
   - 1 tbsp rock salt
   - 1 tbsp brown sugar
   - 3 tsp Chinese five-spice (or a blend of star anise, cloves, cinnamon, fennel seeds and Sichuan pepper)
2. Rinse off the excess salt and pat dry.

## Smoke
1. Build a hot smoker in a wok: scatter the tea leaves, sugar, rice and optional aromatics into the base, then set a wire rack inside:
   - 2-3 spent teabags _leaves only_
   - 3 tsp brown sugar
   - 3 tbsp rice
   - sliced ginger and/or five-spice aromatics (optional)
2. Lay the duck breasts skin-side up on the rack and cover with a lid or foil. Heat to medium.
3. Once smoke appears, reduce the heat to low and cook for [10 minutes](timer:10m), until the duck reaches 50°C (rare).
4. Sear the duck skin-side down in a hot griddle pan for [seven minutes](timer:7m) to crisp the fat — to 57°C for medium-rare or 60°C for medium.
5. Rest, slice, and serve with pancakes and trimmings or plain rice.
