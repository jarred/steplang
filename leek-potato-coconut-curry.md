## Curry
1. Put the oil in a wide, deep pan on a medium heat. Once hot, add the cinnamon, curry leaves and mustard seeds, and let them sizzle for [20–30 seconds](timer:30s):
   - 4 tbsp coconut oil
   - 1 cinnamon stick
   - 10 fresh curry leaves
   - 1 tbsp black mustard seeds
2. Add the aromatics and cook, stirring, for [two to three minutes](timer:3m), until fragrant:
   - 4 garlic cloves _peeled and minced_
   - 2cm ginger _peeled and finely grated_
   - 2 green finger chillies _very finely chopped_ (remove pith and seeds first for less heat)
   - 2 lemongrass stalks _outer husks and top third removed, the rest finely chopped_
   - 1 tsp ground turmeric
3. Add the leeks and salt, and cook for [six to eight minutes](timer:8m) more, until softened:
   - 300g leeks _whites and greens finely sliced, soaked and drained_
   - 1/2 tsp salt
4. Add the potatoes and coconut milk, stir to mix, then add the water and salt. Turn up the heat, bring to a gentle boil, then cover, turn down to a simmer and leave to cook for [15 minutes](timer:15m), until the potatoes are tender:
   - 800g baby potatoes _peeled and cut into 3cm chunks_
   - 600ml coconut milk
   - 250ml water
   - 1¼ tsp salt
5. Take off the lid, let the curry reduce for a further [five minutes](timer:5m), then serve with roti.
   - roti _to serve_
